Googlantis

Design Thinking, Illustration

Zombie Apocalypse!


Googlantis is the concept of an underwater city built for the survivors of a zombie apocalypse. This project was for a Google Designathon held by UC Berkeley's Design Council and the Google+ UX team. On a project team of 4, I rolled out many of the illustrations seen below that we included in our pitch deck.

The competition's prompt outlined the basic characteristics of the zombies, one of which was that they are unable to swim. Drawing on this crucial weakness, we built our entire city underwater and developed special accommodations to this new environment for the survivors.



For example, the city is built out of the giant water-resistant pods to keep out water and maintain a dry and comfortable setting for the survivors.



For safety measures, we accounted for possible zombie infection even underwater. We came up with evacuation pods that launch infected beings back up to surface, maintaining the safety of Googlantis.



To instill normalcy amidst fear and terror of the apocalypse, we stressed higher standards of living by including shopping malls and using technology to make people more secure and connected. A Googlantis citizen can easily take a bike to the Googlantis Mall pod and go shopping at Under Water Outfitters without having to worry about becoming infected!



After working for more than 6 hours, all the teams presented and were judged by the Google+ UX Team. Our team won Best Design and 2nd Place overall.


Takeaways

1. Address values in order of importance.
2. Think about all of the possibilities.
